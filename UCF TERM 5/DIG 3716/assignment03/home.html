Hot Rods: A Brief History Lesson

You may wonder where the term 'hot rod' actually comes from. Most auto enthusiast and hot rodders will agree it came about when people started souping up and racing their cars in the 1930s. Racers would call their rides "hot roadsters" which evolved into what is now called the hot rod. The original hot rods were old Ford Model - Ts, As, or Bs with various types of body and engine modifications to reduce weight and increase speed. After World War II hot rodders used many small military airports that were either abandoned or very rarely used to race on marked courses. Drag racing started from these mile or longer airstips that included up to four lanes of racing at the same time. Hot rodding magazines and associations came about with the rise of hot rodding popularity in the '50s.
